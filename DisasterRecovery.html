Disaster Recovery

What is Disaster Recovery?

Disaster Recovery is the primary insurance strategy for protecting data centers from natural or human-made disasters. It ensures that a business can quickly resume operations or maintain mission-critical functions during or after a disaster. The disaster recovery process includes planning and testing and may involve a separate physical site for recovery operations.

How can we better understand disaster recovery?

From a business continuity perspective, a disaster is any situation that puts an organization's operations at risk. Examples include:

  • Natural disasters
  • Cyber attacks
  • Human errors
  • Machine and hardware failures

How can we prevent these situations?

One way is by using a third-party provider to replicate and host servers in a separate data center. When a disaster strikes, the goal of any disaster recovery plan is to ensure operations run as normally as possible.

Benefits of Disaster Recovery:

  • Improved safety
  • Faster recovery
  • Lower costs
  • Professional expertise

The two main goals of disaster recovery are:

  • Returning affected systems to an operational state as quickly as possible.
  • Minimizing data loss after a catastrophic event or planned outage.

These goals are measured by two key metrics:

  • Recovery Time Objective (RTO): How quickly systems can be restored.
  • Recovery Point Objective (RPO): How much data loss is acceptable.
